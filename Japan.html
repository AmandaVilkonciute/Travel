Japan

Japanese: 日本, Nippon or Nihon and formally 日本国, Nihonkoku.

Information about the country

Geografy

  • Japan comprises 6852 islands extending along the Pacific coast of Asia.
  • It stretches over 3000 km (1900 mi) northeast-southwest from the Sea of Okhotsk to the East China Sea.
  • The Japanese archipelago is 67% forests and 14% agricultural. The primarily rugged and mountainous terrain is restricted for habitation. Thus the habitable zones, mainly in the coastal areas, have very high population densities: Japan is the 40th most densely populated country.

Climate

  • The climate of Japan is predominantly temperate but varies greatly from north to south. The northernmost region, Hokkaido, has a humid continental climate with long, cold winters and very warm to cool summers.
  • The highest temperature ever measured in Japan, 41.1 °C (106.0 °F), was recorded on July 23, 2018, and repeated on August 17, 2020.
  • In the Sea of Japan region on Honshu's west coast, northwest winter winds bring heavy snowfall during winter. In the summer, the region sometimes experiences extremely hot temperatures because of the foehn.

Religion

  • Japan's constitution guarantees full religious freedom.
  • Upper estimates suggest that 84-96 percent of the Japanese population subscribe to Shinto as its indigenous religion.
  • About 90% of those practicing Islam in Japan are foreign-born migrants as of 2016. As of 2018 there were an estimated 105 mosques and 200,000 Muslims in Japan, 43,000 of which were ethnically Japanese.Other minority religions include Hinduism, Judaism, and Baháʼí Faith, as well as the animist beliefs of the Ainu.
